Our story

Born from a
love of slow
living

We started Kindly Offerings because we were tired of buying things that cost the earth to make it.

Not just financially — but literally. Products wrapped in plastics, shipped from factories with no names, made by hands we'd never know. We wanted something different. A shop where every single item could answer the question: where did you come from, and were you made with care?

So we built it. Kindly Offerings is a curated dropshipping store — which means we hold no inventory, work directly with vetted makers and suppliers around the world, and ship straight to you. No warehouse. No waste. Just carefully chosen goods, moving from the hands that made them to the home that needs them.

How it began

A weekend hike
that changed everything

In 2022, Mara and Cole hiked the Ouachita Trail in Arkansas. They'd packed the usual kit — synthetic gear, fast-fashion layers, plastic water bottles — and somewhere around mile 40, sitting at a creek, they started talking about how disconnected the things they carried felt from the place they were in.

They came home and started researching. What they found surprised them: there were hundreds of small makers worldwide — cork farmers in Portugal, bamboo weavers in Vietnam, botanical soap makers in Kyoto — producing extraordinary goods that most people never found, buried beneath mass-market results.

Kindly Offerings was the answer to that problem. A single place to find goods made with real hands, real materials, and a real regard for the world they come from. We choose every product the way you'd choose a gift for someone you love — carefully, with thought for what it took to make it and what it'll mean to receive it.

What we stand by

The principles
behind every product

These aren't marketing words. They're the actual checklist we run every supplier through before a single product goes live on the shop.

01
Material Honesty
We only carry products made from materials that can be named and traced. Natural rubber, organic cotton, cork, beeswax, bamboo, recycled stainless steel. If the ingredient list reads like a chemistry exam, it doesn't come here.
02
Fair Hands
Every supplier we work with must demonstrate fair wages and safe working conditions. We ask, we verify, and we visit when we can. The people who make these things matter as much as what they make.
03
Light Footprint
All orders ship plastic-free in recycled or compostable packaging. We offset 100% of shipping emissions through reforestation partners. Every order plants one tree. We measure what we do to the earth.
04
Genuine Curation
We turn down more products than we accept. There is no algorithm choosing what goes on this shop. Every item is reviewed by a human who has asked: does this belong here? Does it carry kindness in how it was made?
05
Global but Local
We source from 34 countries because good making knows no borders. But we work with regional distribution wherever possible to reduce transit distances — and we always tell you exactly where your order is coming from.
06
Long Life Design
We don't carry trend products. Every item on this shop is designed to last — to be used, repaired, and loved for years. A yoga mat that becomes part of your practice. A mug that earns its place on your shelf. Things worth keeping.

How we source

The Kindly Standard

Every product on this site has passed through the same four-step review. It's slower than just clicking "import" from a catalog. That's the point.

01
Discovery
We find makers through referrals, trade shows, supplier networks, and our own field research. We're always looking for people who make things in ways worth telling a story about.
02
Vetting
We ask every supplier about their materials, labor practices, packaging, and environmental impact. We request documentation, visit when possible, and check third-party certifications.
03
Sampling
We order samples of every product before it goes live. We use them, wash them, test them. If it doesn't hold up, it doesn't ship to you. We've turned down dozens of products at this stage.
04
Ongoing Review
We re-evaluate suppliers annually. Quality can drift. Ownership can change. We stay in relationship with our makers so we know when something has shifted — and act on it if it has.

Our impact

We give 1% of every
sale back to the earth

Through our partnership with One Tree Planted, every order on Kindly Offerings funds the planting of one tree in a reforestation zone matched to the ecosystem of your purchase — Pacific Northwest for camping gear, Atlantic Forest for Brazilian cotton products, Borneo for rubber-based items.

We also offset 100% of shipping emissions through a verified carbon offset program, and all packaging is either recycled, compostable, or returnable. Our goal by 2027 is to be carbon-negative across the full supply chain.

The people behind the products

A few of our makers

We believe you should know who made the things you bring into your home. Here are four of the makers whose work you'll find across our shop.

🇪🇨
Estudio Tierra
Quito, Ecuador
Three generations of potters making stoneware from reclaimed Andean clay. Rosa and her daughters throw every piece by hand in a studio powered entirely by solar. No two mugs are the same — each carries a thumbprint.
🇫🇷
Le Séchoir Sauvage
Provence, France
A husband and wife team who harvest wild grasses and botanicals from the Provençal countryside each summer, dry them in a 200-year-old farmhouse, and ship them in seed-paper packaging you can plant in spring.
🇯🇵
Hinoki Workshop
Kyoto, Japan
A small family soap and body care studio in the hills north of Kyoto, specializing in hinoki cypress — Japan's sacred wood. Cold-pressed, minimal ingredients, and aged the way good things should be.
🇵🇹
Corticeira do Vale
Alentejo, Portugal
A fourth-generation cork farm and workshop in the Alentejo plains. The oak trees here are over 150 years old. Miguel and his team harvest bark by hand every nine years — the tree lives on, untouched at its core.
