How it began

A weekend hike
that changed everything

In 2022, Carla hiked the Ouachita Trail in Arkansas. I'd packed the usual kit — synthetic gear, fast-fashion layers, plastic water bottles — and somewhere around mile 40, sitting at a creek, I started thinking about how disconnected the things they carried felt from the place they were in.

I came home and started researching. What I found surprised them: there were hundreds of small makers worldwide — cork farmers in Portugal, bamboo weavers in Vietnam, botanical soap makers in Kyoto — producing extraordinary goods that most people never found, buried beneath mass-market results.

Kindly Offerings was the answer to that problem. A single place to find goods made with real hands, real materials, and a real regard for the world they come from. I choose every product the way you'd choose a gift for someone you love — carefully, with thought for what it took to make it and what it'll mean to receive it.

"Made with gentle intention."

What I stand by

The principles
behind every product

These aren't marketing words. It's the actual checklist I run every supplier through before a single product goes live on the shop.

01
Material Honesty
I only carry products made from materials that can be named and traced. Natural rubber, organic cotton, cork, beeswax, bamboo, recycled stainless steel. If the ingredient list reads like a chemistry exam, it doesn't come here.
02
Fair Hands
Every supplier I work with must demonstrate fair wages and safe working conditions. I ask, I verify, and I visit when we can. The people who make these things matter as much as what they make.
03
Light Footprint
I ask every supplier about their packaging and environmental practices before listing their products. Light footprint is a standard I hold my makers to.
04
Genuine Curation
I turn down more products than I accept. There is no algorithm choosing what goes on this shop. Every item is reviewed by a human who has asked: does this belong here? Does it carry kindness in how it was made?
05
Global but Local
I source from 34 countries because good making knows no borders. But I work with regional distribution wherever possible to reduce transit distances — and we always tell you exactly where your order is coming from.
06
Long Life Design
We don't carry trend products. Every item on this shop is designed to last — to be used, repaired, and loved for years. A yoga mat that becomes part of your practice. A mug that earns its place on your shelf. Things worth keeping.

How I source

The Kindly Standard

Every product on this site has passed through the same four-step review. It's slower than just clicking "import" from a catalog. That's the point.

01
Discovery
I find makers through referrals, trade shows, supplier networks, and my own field research. I'm always looking for people who make things in ways worth telling a story about.
02
Vetting
I ask every supplier about their materials, labor practices, packaging, and environmental impact. We request documentation, visit when possible, and check third-party certifications.
03
Sampling
I order samples of every product before it goes live. I use them, wash them, test them. If it doesn't hold up, it doesn't ship to you. We've turned down dozens of products at this stage.
04
Ongoing Review
I re-evaluate suppliers annually. Quality can drift. Ownership can change. I stay in relationship with my makers so I know when something has shifted — and act on it if it has.

My impact

We give 1% of every
sale back to the earth

Through my partnership with One Tree Planted, every order on Kindly Offerings funds the planting of one tree in a reforestation zone matched to the ecosystem of your purchase — Pacific Northwest for camping gear, Atlantic Forest for Brazilian cotton products, Borneo for rubber-based items.

We also offset 100% of shipping emissions through a verified carbon offset program, and all packaging is either recycled, compostable, or returnable. My goal by 2027 is to be carbon-negative across the full supply chain.

The people behind the products

A few of my makers

I believe you should know who made the things you bring into your home. Here are four of the makers whose work you'll find across my shop.

🇪🇨
Estudio Tierra
Quito, Ecuador
Three generations of potters making stoneware from reclaimed Andean clay. Rosa and her daughters throw every piece by hand in a studio powered entirely by solar. No two mugs are the same — each carries a thumbprint.
🇫🇷
Le Séchoir Sauvage
Provence, France
A husband and wife team who harvest wild grasses and botanicals from the Provençal countryside each summer, dry them in a 200-year-old farmhouse, and ship them in seed-paper packaging you can plant in spring.
🇯🇵
Hinoki Workshop
Kyoto, Japan
A small family soap and body care studio in the hills north of Kyoto, specializing in hinoki cypress — Japan's sacred wood. Cold-pressed, minimal ingredients, and aged the way good things should be.
🇵🇹
Corticeira do Vale
Alentejo, Portugal
A fourth-generation cork farm and workshop in the Alentejo plains. The oak trees here are over 150 years old. Miguel and his team harvest bark by hand every nine years — the tree lives on, untouched at its core.
